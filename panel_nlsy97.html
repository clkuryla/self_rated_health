This analysis uses panel data from NLSY97 (National Longitudinal Survey Youth 1997). More information and data can be found here: https://www.bls.gov/nls/nlsy97.htm

A total of 6 waves have the variables of interest, so we do not explore first differences, just fixed and random effects.

Outcome variable: health Our outcome variable of interest is self-rated health. In this dataset, the variable has been recoded and named health and ranges from 1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent) as detailed below.

Main predictor: watched_tv Our main predictor variable of interest is how often the participant watches TV. The interpretation ranges from 1 (No TV) to 6 (10+ hours per week). The values are described below. - None - Less than 1 hour a week - 1 to 3 hours a week - 4 to 6 hours a week - 7 to 9 hours a week - 10 hours or more a week

Other predictor: felt_happy How often the respondent felt happy in the last month. This variable has been recoded such that it ranges from: - All of the time - Most of the time - Some of the time - None of the time

Other predictor: depressed How often the respondent was depressed in the last month. This variable has been recoded such that it ranges from: - All of the time - Most of the time - Some of the time - None of the time

## 
##  Hausman Test
## 
## data:  health ~ watched_tv + as.factor(year)
